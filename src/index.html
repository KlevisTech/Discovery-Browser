DISCOVERY BROWSER GUIDE

Help Center

Features, workflows, shortcuts, and troubleshooting in one place.

Version 3.0.0

Browse in focused, independent cards

Discovery Browser keeps the home screen as a control center and opens sites in movable card windows. Each card has navigation, layout, reading, media, bookmark, download, and site-app tools.

DB

Search and launch

Type a web address or search term in the main address field and press Enter. A new card opens using your saved theme, shape, size, and loading animation.

Work across cards

Use Visited Sites to reopen recent cards, Favorites for pinned sites, and the card counter to see how many site windows are active.

Built-in tools

Open local media, read and save articles, review recaps and highlights, manage extensions, track downloads, and customize every new card.

Start here: enter a site in the address field, use the card title bar to navigate, then open Settings to choose the layout that fits your workflow.

Getting started

The main window is your launcher and library; websites open in separate card windows.

  1. 1

    Open a site or search

    Enter a URL or words in the main address field, then press Enter. The selected search engine is used when the text is not a web address.

  2. 2

    Manage active sites

    Visited Sites shows recent destinations and supports up to eight visible entries. Close a card when finished or minimize it into a background bubble when media should continue.

  3. 3

    Pin important sites

    Add up to eight Favorites from the home tray for one-click access. Give a favorite an optional custom title.

  4. 4

    Choose your defaults

    Open Settings to select the search engine, card launch size, per-site layouts, card shape, header theme, loading animation, and audio visualizer.

Card browsing

Every website card is a focused browser window with its own controls.

Navigation and layout

  • Back, Forward, and Reload navigate the active site.
  • Wide View expands the card horizontally.
  • Fullscreen expands the complete site view.
  • Split Screen opens a second site beside the first.
  • Zoom controls change the active page scale.

Save and reopen

  • Bookmark saves the current page into a chosen folder.
  • Read Mode extracts readable article content.
  • Add Site App To Desktop creates or updates a Windows shortcut.
  • The download ring reports active downloads.

Media tools on a card

  • Hover the centered visualizer to open playback controls.
  • Play/pause, seek 10 seconds, mute, and adjust volume.
  • Change visualizer style, side flames, and progress widget.
  • Visual Lock keeps the media visualization available.
  • Cinema and Full modes expand compatible video players.

Compatibility notes

Some protected or cross-origin players cannot be pulled into Cinema mode. Discovery checks for a renderable frame, restores the original page on failure, and recommends the site's own fullscreen control.

Split Screen: click the split button, enter the second URL, and press Enter. Use Ctrl + Shift + S to toggle it quickly.

Media Player

Play local audio and video without opening a website.

Open and organize media

  • Open File selects one supported local media file.
  • Add Folder creates an expandable playlist tray.
  • Video files display native Windows thumbnails and names.
  • Right-click a folder header to remove it.
  • Previous/next track follows folder order.

Playback and visuals

  • Play/pause, seek, volume, mute, and progress controls.
  • Audio displays embedded album art when Windows provides it.
  • Viz toggles the header visualizer and remembers the choice.
  • The sidebar button shows or hides playlists.
  • The controls button hides or reveals player chrome.

Recently played

The home grid stores up to 30 recent items, five per page. Local videos use native thumbnails. Select a card to replay it.

Home and floating playback

Press Home while audio/video is actively playing to keep it in a floating player. If playback is paused or ended, Home clears it instead.

Supported local formats: MP4, WebM, M4V, MOV, MP3, WAV, OGG, AAC, M4A, FLAC, and Opus. Playback still depends on codecs available to Chromium and Windows.

Reader Mode

Turn compatible pages and local text documents into a focused reading view.

Reading controls

  • Back, Forward, and Refresh reader navigation.
  • Choose a reading font and toggle dark mode.
  • Use Fullscreen for distraction-free reading.
  • Images and supported embedded videos remain inline.

Save your work

  • Bookmark Reading Position remembers where you stopped.
  • Save Article stores an offline article payload.
  • Select text to create highlights.
  • Open the highlights tray to revisit saved passages.
  • Recap groups saved articles and linked highlights.

Magic Cursor

Enable Magic Cursor to highlight readable blocks. Move through blocks with the arrow keys, choose an effect from its menu, and press Escape to leave the mode.

Local documents

Use the Upload button on the main screen to select a supported text-based document. PDF upload is currently disabled.

Library and home screen

History

Settings > History lists visited pages. Reopen an entry or clear the complete history.

Bookmarks

Create folders, save pages from card windows, reopen bookmarks, rename folders, and remove entries you no longer need.

Saved Articles and Recap

Reader Mode saves article content locally. Recap provides a dedicated window for browsing saved articles and their highlights.

Downloads

The main and card download rings show active progress. Settings > Downloads lists completed items and lets you clear the list.

Extensions

Open Extensions to switch the built-in Discovery Ad Blocker on or off. It blocks common ads, trackers, pop-ups, and advertising redirects without requiring a download. You can also search the discovery catalog, install supported add-ons, review installed entries, and remove third-party add-ons when needed.

Updates

The bell opens update notices. Check now requests the latest available update information.

Settings and customization

Default browser

Open Windows Default apps and choose Discovery Browser for web links.

Search engine

Select the engine used for text entered in the main address/search field.

Window size and site layouts

Choose Normal, Wide, or Fullscreen for new cards. Add domain-specific rules when a site needs its own launch layout.

Shape and theme

Choose the default card shell and header color theme for cards opened next.

Loading animation

Choose None, Static TV, Water Fill, or Color Fill for new card launches.

Audio visualizer

Enable or disable the animated equalizer on card title bars. Disable it if media playback becomes choppy on lower-powered hardware.

Privacy, safety, and data

Local browser data

History, bookmarks, articles, downloads metadata, favorites, media history, preferences, and sign-in storage are kept in the browser profile on this computer.

Delete All Data

Settings > Delete Data removes local history, bookmarks, passwords, downloads list, extensions, notifications, tabs, and sign-in data. This action is destructive.

Redirect and ad protection

Discovery blocks known ad/tracker hosts and suspicious popup or redirect behavior. Some site functions may be affected when they depend on blocked domains.

Authentication redirects

Sensitive Google and OAuth pages are redirected to an installed Chrome, Edge, or Firefox browser because embedded card sign-in may be rejected. External browser cookies do not automatically transfer back to Discovery. See the Google sign-in help topic.

Downloads and external files

Only open files you trust. Downloaded content is handled by Chromium and stored in your configured Windows download location.

Developer Tools

Developer Tools are available from View for diagnostics. They expose page internals and are intended for advanced troubleshooting.

Why Google sign-in may not complete

Google account authentication is handled differently from ordinary website browsing.

This is an authentication compatibility limitation, not an incorrect password. Google may refuse sign-in pages running inside embedded browser views such as Discovery's website cards.

Why Discovery opens another browser

Discovery treats Google account and OAuth pages as sensitive authentication. Instead of repeatedly loading a flow that Google may reject inside a card, the app offers to open it in an installed Chrome, Edge, or Firefox window.

Why the login may not return to Discovery

The external browser and Discovery use separate browser profiles and cookie stores. Completing Google sign-in externally does not automatically copy that Google session into a Discovery card.

What users should do

  1. Accept the prompt to open the sign-in in Chrome, Edge, or Firefox.
  2. Complete the Google account flow there.
  3. Continue using that service in the external browser when it requires the Google session.
  4. Return to Discovery for pages that do not require embedded Google authentication.

What may still work in Discovery

A site's own username-and-password form may work normally. The limitation mainly affects Sign in with Google, Google OAuth consent, accounts.google.com, and services that require Google cookies inside the card.

What will not fix it

Repeated reloads, Force Reload, changing the card shape, disabling the visualizer, or deleting local data cannot override a provider's embedded-browser policy. Delete All Data should not be used for this issue.

Safety note

Only enter Google credentials on a genuine Google page opened in a browser you trust. Check that the address belongs to google.com before continuing.

Discovery cannot guarantee Google account sign-in inside cards until a provider-supported desktop authentication flow is available.

Troubleshooting

Cinema mode shows black

Wait for the video to start, then try Cinema again. If the compatibility notice appears, use the player's own fullscreen control. Protected and cross-origin video may not support extraction.

Video is choppy

Disable Settings > Audio Visualizer, close unused cards, turn off side flames or progress widgets, and retry playback.

Media thumbnail is missing

Restart after an update. Windows must be able to generate a thumbnail for that codec. Unsupported formats show the fallback tile.

Album art is missing

The audio file must contain artwork that Windows can read. Files without embedded art keep Discovery's generated audio disc.

Google sign-in will not complete

Use the external-browser prompt. Google may reject embedded card authentication, and an external Google session may not transfer back into Discovery. Open the Google sign-in topic for the full explanation.

Resetting a problem

Close and reopen the affected card first. Use Delete All Data only as a last resort because it removes browser profile data.
